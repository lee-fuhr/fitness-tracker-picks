Best budget fitness trackers

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

For most people on a tight budget, the Amazfit Band 7 offers the best blend of features, battery life, and accuracy for the price.

The Fitbit Charge 6 is the pick if you want deeper health insights and a polished app, while the Xiaomi Mi Band 8 is ideal for those who prioritise a tiny, light design.

A fitness tracker doesn’t have to drain your wallet to help you move more, sleep better, and stay motivated. The budget category has matured fast: modern trackers under a few hundred dollars now pack heart-rate monitors, SpO2 sensors, step counting, and multi-day battery life. The challenge is sorting genuine value from gimmicks. To choose the right one, focus on what actually matters for your routine: basic step and sleep tracking is table stakes; look for a comfortable strap, a screen you can read in sunlight, and a companion app that doesn’t feel like a chore. Battery life varies hugely – some need a charge every few days, others last weeks. We’ve sifted through owner feedback and expert reviews to find the three models that deliver the most for the least.

Best for specific needs

Best for all-day wear comfort

If you plan to wear your tracker 24/7 – including during sleep – size and weight matter more than most specs. The Xiaomi Mi Band 8 is the clear winner here: its slim capsule design and soft strap disappear on the wrist, making it easy to forget you even have it on. The Amazfit Band 7 and Fitbit Charge 6 are both comfortable, but the Xiaomi’s lower profile edges ahead for uninterrupted wear. Our pick: Xiaomi Mi Band 8.

Best for detailed health insights

When you want more than just steps and sleep, the Fitbit Charge 6 delivers. Its companion app surfaces long-term trends, Daily Readiness Scores, stress management, and even ECG (in select regions). The trade-off is that some of the most useful analysis sits behind a premium subscription. Still, if you’re willing to pay, no other budget tracker gives you this level of guided health feedback. Our pick: Fitbit Charge 6.

Best for battery life and screen quality

The Amazfit Band 7 strikes an uncommon balance: a vivid, always-on AMOLED display that rivals far pricier wearables, paired with battery life that routinely exceeds two weeks. That combination is rare at any price. Most trackers force you to choose between a good screen and long battery – the Amazfit gives you both, making it the ideal pick for people who dislike frequent charging. Our pick: Amazfit Band 7.

Our verdict

The budget tracker space is crowded, but the right choice comes down to your priorities. If you want the best overall value with a great screen and stellar battery, go with the Amazfit Band 7. If you crave deeper health data and onboard GPS, the Fitbit Charge 6 justifies its higher price. And if comfort and minimalism are everything, the Xiaomi Mi Band 8 is tiny, light, and perfectly capable. Any of these three will get you moving for far less than a smartwatch – pick the one that matches your daily habits.
